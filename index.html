Maya Angelou

Poet & Novelist

April 4, 1928 - May 28, 2014

Maya Angelou, original name Marguerite Johnson was born in St.Louis,Missouri.Her parents divorced when she was three years old and her and her brother went to live with her grandmother in Stamps, Arkansas.At age eight, she was raped by her mother's boyfriend. Her uncles kicked the culprit to death.Frightened by the power of speaking up, Maya Angelou chose not to speak for the next five years

In 1940 she moved to San Franscico with her mother and brother.Angelou began taking dance lessons and eventually auditioned for a professional theatre. However she had to pause on that career as she had her son at age 16. She moved to San Diego, where she worked as a night club waitress. Here, she became involved with drugs and prostitution and danced in strip clubs. She was later discovered by a theatre group.She auditioned for an international tour of Porgy and Bess and won a role. She toured 22 countries with the group from 1954 to 1955.

In 1955, she moved to New York and became friends with prominent Harlem writers and got involved with the civil rights movement.1961 she moved to Egypt with a boyfriend and edited for the Arab Observer.She later left her boyfriend and moved to Ghana where a car accident severely injured her son. While caring for her son she took a job at the African Review. Her writing and personal development flourished under the African cultural renaissance.

She returned to the US and began publishing her multivolume autobiography, starting with the I Know Why The Caged Birds Sings. Four more books appeared during the next two decades as well as several books of poetry.

In 1981, she was appointed Professor of American Studies at the Wake Forest University in Winston in Salem. She was nominated for several important awards and read a poem written for the occasion at president Clinton's inauguration.

Maya Angelou died on May 28, 2014 in North Carolina.

Phenomenal Woman

- Maya Angelou

Pretty women wonder where my secret lies.
I'm not cute or built to suit a fashion model's size
But when I start to tell them,
They think I'm telling lies.
I say,
It's in the reach of my arms
The span of my hips,
The stride of my step,
The curl of my lips.
I'm a woman
Phenomenally.
Phenomenal woman,
That's me.
